## Why This Exists

The traditional PM playbook — discover, define, design, develop, deliver — was built on assumptions that vibe coding just made obsolete.

When a working prototype can be scaffolded in an afternoon, the bottleneck is no longer *building*. It's **deciding**. And that distinction has radical implications for every PM who thinks their job is still about writing requirements.

This playbook exists to close the gap between how product management is taught and how it actually needs to work in AI-native organizations — whether you're a traditional PM making the transition or a new grad entering the field for the first time.

## The Two Frameworks

This playbook introduces two original PM frameworks — both rooted in proven AI engineering disciplines and designed to replace the traditional five-stage lifecycle.

### ⚡ DARE — For Innovation Teams
> *Rooted in Eric Ries's Build-Measure-Learn loop + MLOps continuous feedback cycles*

When you have autonomy to choose what to build.

```
D — Decide First        → Form conviction before research. Use AI to validate in hours.
A — Act Before Ready    → Build a working surface in 48hrs. Not a wireframe. Reality.
R — Read at Scale       → Let AI parse live behavioral signals. Skip the usability study.
E — Expand or Erase     → Binary. Clear signal: ship wider. Weak signal: kill clean.
  — Own the Outcome     → Document every bet. AI maintains the log. You own the judgment.
```

### 🏛️ ALIGN — For Enterprise Teams
> *Rooted in LLMOps lifecycle management + AgentOps governance principles*

When business leaders hand you the mandate and you execute.

```
A — Anchor the Intent   → Turn exec mandate into a signed one-page intent brief.
L — Lay the Constraints → Map every blocker before build. Regulatory, integration, approvals.
I — Iterate in the Open → Weekly demos to stakeholders. Make change cheap, not impossible.
G — Gate with Purpose   → One owner, one question, 48-hr SLA per gate. No theatre.
N — Normalize + Narrate → Document what shipped. Translate features into business outcomes.
```

## Agent Monitoring Layer

Both frameworks include an **AI agent monitoring layer** — inspired by AgentOps observability principles — that provides:

- 🚦 **Stage duration tracking** against defined SLAs
- ⚠️ **Automatic blocker detection** via commit activity and communication signals
- 📋 **Auto-generated stakeholder updates** in plain executive language
- 🚨 **Risk flagging with recommended actions** — escalate, pause, or accelerate
- 📅 **Leadership convene triggers** when risk thresholds are crossed

> *Before agents: PM spends 4hrs Friday assembling a status deck from 6 sources.*
> *After agents: Leadership update is auto-drafted by Monday 8am. PM reviews in 5 minutes.*

## How to Use This Playbook

**If you're just starting out:**
1. Go to the [live playbook](https://therealdealio.github.io/ai-pm-playbook)
2. Select your path (Traditional PM or New Grad)
3. Read Chapter 01 and complete Exercise 1.1 before moving on
4. Don't skip Chapter 02 — the 30-60-90 day plan is where most people get stuck

**If you're preparing for interviews:**
1. Jump to Chapter 07 first
2. Build your AI PM thesis statement (the final exercise)
3. Then work backward through Chapters 03 and 06

**If you're already an AI PM:**
1. Use the DARE/ALIGN checklists in Chapter 03 as a working tool
2. Steal the exec status update template from Chapter 05
3. Share the article with your team — it's a useful forcing function for alignment conversations
